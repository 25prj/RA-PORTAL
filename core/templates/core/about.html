About Type Approval & Dealership License

Understanding the processes for Type Approval and Dealership License issued by the NCA, Ghana.

What is Type Approval?

Type Approval is a certification process conducted by the National Communications Authority (NCA) to ensure that electronic communication equipment meets the required technical standards and regulations before being sold or used in Ghana.

This process is mandatory for all telecommunications and electronic devices, including mobile phones, radios, and other wireless equipment, to ensure they are safe, efficient, and compatible with Ghana's communication networks.

When to Apply for Type Approval

You need to apply for Type Approval if:

  • You are importing or manufacturing electronic communication devices for sale in Ghana.
  • You are a dealer or distributor of telecommunications equipment.
  • You want to ensure your devices comply with NCA regulations.

Type Approval Process

  1. Submit an application to the NCA with the required documentation (e.g., technical specifications, test reports).
  2. Pay the applicable fees for the Type Approval process.
  3. The NCA reviews the application and conducts tests on the equipment.
  4. If the equipment meets the standards, the NCA issues a Type Approval certificate.

What is a Dealership License?

A Dealership License is a permit issued by the NCA to individuals or businesses that wish to sell or distribute electronic communication equipment in Ghana. This license ensures that dealers operate legally and adhere to NCA regulations.

When to Apply for a Dealership License

You need to apply for a Dealership License if:

  • You are a retailer or distributor of electronic communication equipment.
  • You want to sell devices that have already received Type Approval from the NCA.
  • You are setting up a business to sell telecommunications equipment.

Dealership License Process

  1. Submit an application to the NCA with the required documents (e.g., business registration, proof of Type Approval for devices).
  2. Pay the applicable fees for the Dealership License.
  3. The NCA reviews the application and conducts an inspection of your business premises (if necessary).
  4. If approved, the NCA issues a Dealership License.
